Explore the Star Systems

Discover the wonders of space, from the planets in our Solar System to the vastness of the Milky Way galaxy.

TRAPPIST-1 System
A fascinating system with seven Earth-sized planets, three of which reside in the habitable zone, offering unique research opportunities for scientists studying potential life-supporting conditions.
Kepler-186 System
This system contains the first confirmed Earth-sized exoplanet in the habitable zone of another star, marking a pivotal moment in the quest for Earth-like worlds beyond our solar system.
Kepler-62 System
Known for its two planets located within the habitable zone, this system is a prime candidate for studying the characteristics of potentially habitable worlds.
Alpha Centauri System
The closest star system to Earth, Proxima Centauri hosts a planet in the habitable zone, making it an exciting target for future astronomical research on the potential for life beyond our solar system.
Kepler-452 System
Often referred to as "Earth's cousin," this system includes a super-Earth in the habitable zone, stirring interest in its geological and atmospheric properties.
GJ 1214 System
GJ 1214 b is noted for its potentially watery atmosphere, making the entire system a focal point for studies of exoplanetary atmospheres and habitability.
GJ 3470 System
With a close-in super-Earth, this system provides a unique setting to understand the effects of stellar radiation on planetary atmospheres.
LHS 1140 System
This system features LHS 1140 b, which is of particular interest due to its size and potential for detailed atmospheric study.
HAT-P-7 System
Known for its hot Jupiter exoplanet, this system presents an exciting opportunity to study atmospheric phenomena in extreme environments.
